TALKING POINTS

1. Open with acknowledgment:
"Paid media is working. CPA is down 13%, volume is up, the channel reallocation is smart. We want to keep that relationship strong."

2. Frame the SEO problem:
"At $90K/year, we're paying for advisory work - content outlines, PageSpeed screenshots, keyword tracking. Our team writes every blog, implements every fix. Multiple SOW deliverables are missing: no CTR analysis, no CRO testing, one competitor audit in 18 months, structured data that was never deployed. We need the retainer to reflect the actual scope of work."

3. Hit the organic growth number:
"The Annual Review claims 29% organic growth. But your own dashboard shows you cut branded Google Ads by 96% - from $28,600 to $1,200/month. That traffic migrated to organic in GA4. When you strip that out, organic sessions are only up 3.2% YoY. That's $90K for 3% growth."

4. The local gap:
"We have 20+ locations and zero Google Business Profile optimization, zero Yelp strategy, zero TripAdvisor management. For a multi-location entertainment business, that's the highest-impact SEO work - and it's completely absent."

5. The ask:
"We want to renegotiate SEO from $7,500 to $2,500–$3,500/month. We'd also like a detailed hours breakdown for the last quarter, and a clear answer on which SOW deliverables are actually being produced."

6. If they push back:

  • "Rankings take time" → "You've had 20 months. National terms haven't moved. Location terms were likely strong before you started."
  • "Organic revenue is up 29%" → "After adjusting for the branded search cut, real organic growth is ~3%. We have the dashboard data."
  • "We provide strategic value" → "Strategy without execution is a to-do list. Your own competitor audit recommendations took 16 months to act on."

  • No depth beyond SEMrush exports: Missing backlink comparison, technical SEO comparison, content velocity analysis, conversion path comparison, review/social benchmarking, and keyword gap analysis.

SECTION 5: THE LOCAL PLATFORM GAP

The biggest missing piece in Silverback's SEO program

Muse Paintbar operates 20+ physical locations across the East Coast. For a multi-location business, local platform optimization is arguably the highest-impact SEO activity - it directly drives the Google Map Pack, Yelp discovery, and booking conversions. There is zero evidence Silverback is managing or advising on any of these platforms.

Google Business Profile (GBP) - Not Being Managed

GBP is the #1 local SEO channel. It controls Map Pack rankings, drives calls/website clicks/direction requests, and is the first thing customers see for local searches. For 20+ locations, ongoing GBP management should include:

  • Regular Google Posts (events, promotions, seasonal paintings, new offerings)
  • Photo and video uploads per location
  • Q&A monitoring and proactive FAQ seeding
  • Review response strategy and templates
  • Category, attribute, and service optimization per location
  • Hours updates (holidays, special events, seasonal changes)
  • GBP performance reporting per location (impressions, clicks, calls, direction requests)
  • Competitor GBP benchmarking

Evidence from Silverback: None. No GBP strategy, no GBP metrics in monthly reports, no posting calendar, no review management plan.

Yelp - Not Being Managed (Despite Active Spend)

Yelp is especially relevant for Muse because paint-and-sip sits at the intersection of entertainment + food/drink - exactly where Yelp has the strongest user intent. Yelp pages also rank on Google page 1 for "[city] paint and sip" searches.

The spend reality: Muse was paying $3,650–$4,650/month on Yelp ads through Oct 2024 (~$44K/year), reduced to $720/month in Nov 2024. Even at reduced spend, Yelp organic presence across 20+ locations is completely unmanaged. For 20+ locations, Yelp management should include:

  • Optimized business descriptions with local keywords
  • Professional photo management per location
  • Review monitoring and response strategy
  • Yelp Deals or Check-in Offers to drive foot traffic
  • Yelp Connect posts (Yelp's equivalent of Google Posts)
  • Category optimization
  • Tracking Yelp as a referral source in analytics

Evidence from Silverback: None.

SECTION 9: RECOMMENDATION

Tier 1: Keep As-Is

Paid Media ($10,000/month): Performance justifies the spend. CPA declining, volume growing, strategic channel optimization is evident.

Tier 2: Renegotiate Significantly

SEO ($7,500/month → $2,500–$3,500/month): The current deliverables do not justify $90K/year. A reduced retainer reflecting the actual advisory workload would be fair. Alternatively, explore replacing with:

  • AI tools for content briefs (Claude, SurferSEO, Clearscope): ~$200-500/month
  • A freelance SEO strategist for monthly audits and keyword research: ~$2,000-3,000/month
  • PageSpeed Insights and Google Search Console (free) for technical monitoring

Tier 3: Evaluate

Analytics ($1,000/month): Low cost but unclear value. What exactly is delivered for $12K/year?

Creative Strategy ($10,000/quarter): Downgraded from production to concepts only. Ten ideas and one call per quarter for $10K warrants scrutiny.
