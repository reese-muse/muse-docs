What's working:

  • Organic Search is our #1 channel - 40% of revenue ($674K/month), meaning ~60% of our revenue comes from non-paid sources. Healthy foundation.
  • Paid media is efficient - $95.9K/month spend driving 11,800 purchases at $8.07 CPA, declining 13% YoY. The team is strategically shifting from branded search to prospecting channels.
  • Email (Klaviyo) is high-ROI infrastructure - $67K/year platform cost, 68.5% open rates, 287K email recipients. Recently reduced from $85K/year with no impact.

Where we're optimizing:

  • SEO is overpriced. We pay $90K/year for advisory-only work (our team does all implementation). Renegotiating down to $30–42K - saving $48–60K/year.
  • Local presence is unmanaged. Google Business Profiles and Yelp across 20+ locations have no optimization strategy. High-impact gap we're addressing.
  • Total vendor savings in progress: $68–100K/year without impacting revenue-driving capabilities.

Bottom line: Marketing is revenue-positive with a diversified channel mix. The two biggest spend items (paid media + Klaviyo) are delivering strong results. We've identified the underperformers and are actively renegotiating - not cutting capability, cutting waste.

What's Working

  • Paid media management is strong. CPA declining year-over-year while volume grows. Smart channel reallocation - shifted spend from Performance Max to non-brand Google and Meta prospecting. Google delivering $5.83 CPA across 9,100+ purchases/month.
  • Location-specific keyword rankings are dominant. #1 for "paint and sip nyc" (5,400 searches), "paint and sip boston" (1,000 searches), and most city-specific variations.
  • Revenue is growing. +7% YoY overall, beating the 6% target. Blog traffic growing significantly from a low base.

What We're Addressing

  • SEO is overpriced for deliverables received. At $90K/year, we're paying for advisory work (content outlines, PageSpeed screenshots, keyword tracking) that our internal team implements. Silverback produces zero finished content - our marketing team writes all blogs, our developer handles all technical implementation.
  • Multiple SOW deliverables are missing or incomplete. Structured data produced for only 6 pages (never deployed). One competitor audit in 18 months. No CTR analysis, no CRO testing (despite projecting $789K from it), no 3-month rolling plan. Recommendations from their own Oct 2024 competitor audit took 16 months to begin acting on.
  • Local platform management is absent. No Google Business Profile optimization, no Yelp strategy, no TripAdvisor management across 20+ locations. For a multi-location entertainment business, these are the highest-impact local SEO channels.
  • National keyword rankings are weak. #20 for "paint and sip" (40,500 monthly searches), #36 for "sip and paint" (22,200). These high-volume terms represent significant untapped organic traffic.

Our Plan

Paid media ($120K/year): Keep as-is. Performance justifies the spend and shows strategic depth.
SEO ($90K/year): Renegotiating down to $2,500–$3,500/month ($30K–$42K/year). The advisory-only model doesn't justify $90K. We're evaluating supplementing with AI tools for content briefs and a focused local SEO strategy covering GBP, Yelp, and review management. Potential savings: $48K–$60K/year.
Analytics + Creative ($52K/year): Under review. Analytics at $12K/year has unclear deliverables. Creative strategy was downgraded from production to concepts-only but still costs $40K/year for 10 ideas and one call per quarter.

$1.75M in Klaviyo-attributed revenue over 30 days on ~$5,590/month in platform cost. That's a ~314:1 revenue-to-platform-cost ratio. Even with generous attribution caveats (Klaviyo attributes broadly via confirmation emails and flows), the email channel is a major revenue driver.

What's Working

  • High engagement rates. 68.5% campaign open rate and 61–72% flow open rates are well above industry averages (~20–25% for retail/entertainment). This indicates a healthy, engaged list.
  • Flows outperform campaigns significantly. Automated flows drive 4.6–9.6% click rates vs. 1.0% for campaigns - a 5–10x multiplier. This is expected (flows are triggered by user actions) but shows the automations are well-configured.
  • Public Sessions dominate revenue. 89.7% of Klaviyo-attributed revenue comes from public session confirmations, confirming this is the core business line.
  • Low spam complaints. 0.007% spam rate is excellent - well below the 0.1% threshold that triggers deliverability issues.
  • Recent cost optimization. Downgraded plan from ~$7,077/month to ~$5,590/month (~$18K annual savings) without impacting functionality.

What We're Watching

  • Campaign click rate is low. 1.0% click rate on campaigns (vs. 4.6–9.6% on flows) suggests campaign content could be optimized - better segmentation, more personalized offers, stronger CTAs. There's upside here.
  • SMS is underutilized. Only 2,605 SMS recipients vs. 287,452 email recipients. SMS is a ~1% penetration rate. For a business with time-sensitive event inventory (tonight's empty seats, last-minute openings), SMS could drive meaningful incremental bookings.
  • Unsubscribe rate warrants monitoring. 0.48% per campaign cycle is slightly elevated. Not alarming, but with ~287K recipients, that's ~1,380 unsubs per 30-day cycle. Send frequency and segmentation should be reviewed to protect list health.
  • Revenue attribution is broad. The $1.75M Klaviyo figure includes confirmation notification emails (booking confirmations triggered by purchases). For comparison, GA4 last-click attribution credits Email with $17,751 in January 2026 revenue (1.1% of total) and SMS with $2,301 (0.1%). The true incremental value of email/SMS is between these figures - GA4 undercounts (ignores assisted conversions) while Klaviyo overcounts (credits transactional emails). We should set up proper attribution to isolate email-influenced revenue from transactional notifications.
  • Private party revenue through Klaviyo is modest. $159K (9.1%) vs. $1.57M public sessions. Given private parties are higher-margin, there's an opportunity to build dedicated email/SMS flows for private event lead nurturing.

Our Plan

Keep Klaviyo. At ~$67K/year (reduced from ~$85K), it's a core infrastructure platform driving high engagement and supporting $1.75M+ in monthly attributed revenue. The ROI is clear even with attribution caveats.
Optimize: Improve campaign click rates through better segmentation, grow SMS list for last-minute inventory fills, build private party nurture flows, and set up proper attribution to separate transactional confirmation revenue from campaign-driven incremental revenue.
